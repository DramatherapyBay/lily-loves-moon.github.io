What is Dramatherapy?

Dramatherapy is a specialized form of psychotherapy that harnesses the power of drama, theater, and creative expression to promote healing and personal growth.

A Holistic Approach to Healing

Unlike traditional talk therapy, dramatherapy engages the whole person – mind, body, and spirit – creating opportunities for profound healing and self-discovery.

Through the safe medium of creative play and storytelling, individuals can explore difficult emotions and develop greater self-awareness. This approach allows clients to safely explore experiences through techniques such as role-play, improvisation, and storytelling.

Evidence-Based Practice

Dramatherapy is recognized by the Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC) and is used in hospitals, schools, and private practice settings worldwide. The process is conducted by trained professionals working collaboratively with clients.

Dramatherapy

Therapy Approach

Creative, experiential therapy providing psychological distance to explore body image, food, and emotions. Uses story, role-play, movement, and imagery.

Structure

Sessions may include talking therapy, storytelling, role-play, movement, or creative expression.

Time Frame

Usually weekly, over several months depending on client needs and goals.

Suitable For

Clients who find direct discussion triggering, those with neurodivergence (ASD, ADHD), or restrictive/emotional eating patterns.

CBT-informed

Therapy Approach

Structured, practical strategies adapted from CBT principles. Focuses on thoughts, emotions, and behaviours to reduce unhelpful patterns.

Structure

Sessions are structured, collaborative, and goal-focused. Includes assessment, goal setting, and progress review.

Time Frame

Typically 10 sessions standard, extendable if more intensive treatment is required.

Suitable For

Clients motivated to actively engage, including Bulimia Nervosa and Binge Eating Disorder.

Guided Self-Help

Therapy Approach

Evidence-informed self-help supported by therapist guidance. Focus on understanding eating patterns and building coping strategies.

Structure

Short, structured therapy sessions combined with self-help materials. Therapist provides guidance and review.

Time Frame

Typically 8–12 sessions, depending on client pace and needs.

Suitable For

Clients with Binge Eating Disorder, Bulimia Nervosa, or early/milder eating difficulties.

A Compassionate Approach to Weight and Wellbeing

Many dieting programmes focus on quick, short-term weight loss. Our approach is different. We understand that some people may wish to lose weight, but our work focuses on helping you develop a healthy, sustainable, and compassionate relationship with food, eating, and your body — rather than chasing rapid, temporary results.

As eating patterns stabilise and health behaviours improve, some people may notice gradual, natural changes in weight over time. However, we do not promise or promote weight-loss outcomes.
