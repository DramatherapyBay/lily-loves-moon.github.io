About Dramatherapy

Dramatherapy is a gentle, creative path to emotional well-being. It is a form of psychological therapy that uses our natural capacity for imagination and play to help us understand ourselves better.

Sometimes, life's challenges are hard to put into words. Dramatherapy provides a "creative language" to help you express feelings, process experiences, and find new ways forward in a safe, supportive environment.

A Creative Partnership

The most important thing to know is that you do not need to be an actor or an artist. There is no "audience" and no "performance."

Dramatherapy is not about being "good" at drama; it is about using simple creative tools to explore your inner world. You are never forced to use a specific art form. Instead, different options are offered to you as invitations—you choose what feels right for you on any given day.

Your Creative Toolkit

  • Storytelling & Metaphor: Using myths or making up stories to explore life themes.
  • Objects & Sand Play: Placing small figures in sand or using objects to represent feelings.
  • Creative Writing & Drawing: Using journals, poetry, or simple images to express a thought.
  • Images & Cards: Using visual prompts to spark a conversation or a feeling.
  • Music & Sound: Using rhythm or melody to connect with your emotions.
  • Body Movement: Gentle ways to notice how your body holds stress or strength.
  • Role Play & Enactment: Exploring a situation from a new perspective in a safe way.

What Happens in a Session?

While every session is tailored to your specific needs, we generally follow a structured rhythm to ensure you feel grounded and safe:

Phase What We Do
Check-in A quiet moment to land in the space and share how you are feeling.
Warm-up A light activity to help you transition from your busy day into a creative mindset.
Main Activity Using a creative tool (like a story or sand tray) to explore a specific theme.
Reflection A time to talk about what happened and what it might mean for you.
De-roling A simple step to "leave the work behind" so you can return to your daily life.
Check-out A final grounding moment to ensure you feel ready to leave the session.
