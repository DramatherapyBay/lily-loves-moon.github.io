Dramatherapy

Therapy Approach

Creative, experiential therapy providing psychological distance to explore body image, food, and emotions. Uses story, role-play, movement, and imagery.

Structure

Sessions may include talking therapy, storytelling, role-play, movement, or creative expression. Participation is always optional; clients remain in full control.

Time Frame

Usually weekly, over several months depending on client needs and goals.

Suitable For

Clients who find direct discussion triggering, those with neurodivergence (ASD, ADHD), anxiety, or restrictive/emotional eating patterns. Also suitable for Anorexia Nervosa.

Home Tasks / Practice

Reflection activities, noticing emotional patterns, grounding exercises, optional creative practice between sessions.

CBT-informed

Therapy Approach

Structured, practical strategies adapted from CBT principles, integrated under a dramatherapy framework. Focuses on thoughts, emotions, and behaviours.

Structure

Sessions are structured, collaborative, and goal-focused. Includes assessment, goal setting, skill practice, and progress review.

Time Frame

Typically 10 sessions standard, extendable if more intensive treatment is required.

Suitable For

Clients motivated to actively engage, including those with Bulimia Nervosa, Binge Eating Disorder, and some presentations of Anorexia Nervosa.

Home Tasks / Practice

Monitoring thoughts and behaviours, practising coping strategies, adjusting routines, reflection exercises between sessions.

Guided Self-Help

Therapy Approach

Evidence-informed self-help exercises supported by therapist guidance. Focus on understanding eating patterns and building sustainable recovery skills.

Structure

Short, structured therapy sessions combined with self-help materials. Therapist provides guidance, support, and progress review.

Time Frame

Typically 8–12 sessions, depending on client pace and needs.

Suitable For

Binge Eating Disorder, Bulimia Nervosa, emotional eating, or milder eating difficulties. Requires motivation to work independently.

Home Tasks / Practice

Completing structured exercises, monitoring thoughts and behaviours, practising new routines, and self-compassion activities.

A Compassionate Approach to Weight and Wellbeing

Many dieting programmes focus on quick, short-term weight loss. Our approach is different. We understand that some people may wish to lose weight, but our work focuses on helping you develop a healthy, sustainable, and compassionate relationship with food, eating, and your body.

As eating patterns stabilise and health behaviours improve, some people may notice gradual, natural changes in weight over time. However, we do not promise or promote weight-loss outcomes.
