What is Dramatherapy?

Dramatherapy is a specialized form of psychotherapy that harnesses the power of drama, theater, and creative expression to promote healing and personal growth.

A Holistic Approach to Healing

Unlike traditional talk therapy, dramatherapy engages the whole person – mind, body, and spirit – creating opportunities for profound healing and self-discovery. Through the safe medium of creative play and storytelling, individuals can explore difficult emotions and develop greater self-awareness.

This innovative therapeutic approach allows clients to safely explore their emotions, experiences, and relationships through techniques such as role-play, improvisation, storytelling, and creative movement.

Evidence-Based Practice

Dramatherapy is recognized by the Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC) and is used in hospitals, schools, community centers, and private practice settings worldwide.

Professional Recognition: HCPC Registered

Dramatherapy

Therapy Approach

Creative, experiential therapy providing psychological distance to explore body image, food, and emotions. Uses story, role-play, movement, and imagery.

Structure

Sessions include talking therapy, storytelling, role-play, or movement. Participation is always optional; clients remain in full control.

Time Frame

Usually weekly, over several months.

Suitable For

Triggering direct talk, neurodivergence (ASD, ADHD), anxiety, Anorexia Nervosa.

Home Tasks

Reflection, emotional noticing, grounding exercises.

CBT-informed

Therapy Approach

Structured, practical strategies adapted from CBT principles. Focuses on thoughts, emotions, and behaviours to reduce unhelpful patterns.

Structure

Goal-focused and collaborative. Includes assessment, skill practice, and progress review.

Time Frame

Typically 10 sessions standard.

Suitable For

Bulimia Nervosa, Binge Eating Disorder, motivated clients.

Home Tasks

Monitoring thoughts, coping strategies, adjusting routines.

Guided Self-Help

Therapy Approach

Evidence-informed self-help exercises supported by therapist guidance. Focus on understanding patterns and sustainable recovery skills.

Structure

Short structured sessions combined with self-help materials.

Time Frame

Typically 8–12 sessions.

Suitable For

Binge Eating Disorder, emotional eating, milder difficulties.

Home Tasks

Completing exercises, self-compassion activities.

A Compassionate Approach to Weight and Wellbeing

Many dieting programmes focus on quick, short-term weight loss. Our approach is different. We understand that some people may wish to lose weight, but our work focuses on helping you develop a healthy, sustainable, and compassionate relationship with food, eating, and your body — rather than chasing rapid, temporary results.

As eating patterns stabilise and health behaviours improve, some people may notice gradual, natural changes in weight over time. However, we do not promise or promote weight-loss outcomes. Your emotional, psychological, and physical wellbeing is our priority.
